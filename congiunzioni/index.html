Congiunzioni — Italian Conjunctions

Coordinating, subordinating, and correlative conjunctions — which mood each one requires, with tables, examples, and common pitfalls
Coordinating Conjunctions (Congiunzioni Coordinative)
Join two independent clauses or words of equal grammatical rank. They never change the mood of the verb.
Overview
Coordinating conjunctions connect words, phrases, or clauses that have the same syntactic function. They do not trigger any special verb mood — both sides keep whatever mood they would have on their own.
Conjunction Meaning Type Usage Note
e / ed and copulative Use ed before words starting with e- (ed esempio, ed era); optional before other vowels
o / oppure or disjunctive oppure is slightly more emphatic; both are interchangeable in most contexts
ma but adversative Most common adversative; introduces a direct contrast or correction
però however, but adversative Can appear mid-sentence or at the start; slightly softer than ma
tuttavia nevertheless, however adversative More formal register; common in written Italian
né...né neither...nor copulative-negative Requires non before the verb: Non mangio né carne né pesce
anzi on the contrary, actually adversative Corrects or strengthens the previous statement; often introduces something stronger
dunque / quindi / perciò therefore, so conclusive quindi is most versatile; perciò emphasises cause-effect; dunque is slightly formal
allora so, then conclusive Very common in speech; also means "then" in temporal sense
infatti in fact, indeed explanatory Confirms or explains the previous statement; not adversative like English "actually"
cioè that is, namely explanatory Introduces a clarification or reformulation of what was just said
Examples
e Marco studia economia e sua sorella studia medicina all'università di Bologna.
Marco studies economics and his sister studies medicine at the University of Bologna.
ed Ho comprato il pane ed erano già le otto di sera quando sono uscito dal negozio.
I bought the bread and it was already eight in the evening when I left the shop.
ma Vorrei venire alla festa, ma purtroppo devo lavorare fino a tardi questo venerdì.
I would like to come to the party, but unfortunately I have to work late this Friday.
però Il ristorante era molto caro; il cibo, però, era davvero eccellente e ne valeva la pena.
The restaurant was very expensive; the food, however, was truly excellent and it was worth it.
né...né Non ho né il tempo né la voglia di andare a quella riunione noiosissima.
I have neither the time nor the desire to go to that extremely boring meeting.
anzi Non era triste per la notizia, anzi, sembrava ancora più felice di prima.
He was not sad about the news; on the contrary, he seemed even happier than before.
quindi Ha piovuto tutto il giorno, quindi abbiamo deciso di restare a casa e guardare un film.
It rained all day, so we decided to stay home and watch a movie.
infatti Maria parla benissimo il francese; infatti, ha vissuto a Parigi per oltre cinque anni.
Maria speaks French very well; in fact, she lived in Paris for over five years.
ed vs e: The form ed is used primarily before words beginning with e- to avoid the awkward repetition of the same vowel sound (Marco ed Elena). Before other vowels it is optional and increasingly considered old-fashioned. Never use ed before consonants.
Subordinating Conjunctions — Indicativo
These conjunctions introduce dependent clauses that take the indicativo because they express facts, certainties, or real events.
Conjunctions That Take Indicativo
When the subordinate clause states something factual, temporal, or causal, Italian uses the indicativo. These conjunctions introduce real situations, established reasons, or definite time references.
Conjunction Meaning Category Mood Required
che that declarative indicativo (after verbs of certainty: sapere, vedere, notare)
perché because causal indicativo when explaining a reason or cause
quando when temporal indicativo for definite time references
mentre while, whereas temporal / adversative indicativo — simultaneous or contrasting actions
se if conditional indicativo for real, possible conditions (periodo ipotetico della realtà)
siccome since, as causal indicativo — always introduces a known cause; placed at sentence start
dato che / visto che given that, seeing that causal indicativo — presupposes a known, accepted fact
dopo che after temporal indicativo (usually passato prossimo or trapassato prossimo)
finché until, as long as temporal indicativo for factual duration; sometimes + non for "until"
anche se even if, even though concessive indicativo when the situation is real and established
appena as soon as temporal indicativo (usually passato prossimo in subordinate clause)
Examples
che So che Marco arriva domani mattina con il treno delle nove e mezza.
I know that Marco arrives tomorrow morning on the nine-thirty train.
perché Sono rimasto a casa perché avevo la febbre alta e non mi sentivo per niente bene.
I stayed home because I had a high fever and I did not feel well at all.
quando Quando ero piccolo, andavo sempre al mare con i miei nonni durante l'estate.
When I was little, I always went to the sea with my grandparents during the summer.
mentre Mentre io cucinavo la cena, mia moglie leggeva un libro sul divano del soggiorno.
While I was cooking dinner, my wife was reading a book on the living room sofa.
se Se domani piove, prenderemo l'autobus invece di andare a piedi fino alla stazione.
If it rains tomorrow, we will take the bus instead of walking to the station.
siccome Siccome non c'era più posto in treno, abbiamo dovuto prendere l'autobus per Firenze.
Since there was no more room on the train, we had to take the bus to Florence.
dato che Dato che hai già finito i compiti, puoi uscire a giocare con i tuoi amici.
Given that you have already finished your homework, you can go out and play with your friends.
appena Appena sono arrivato alla stazione, ho chiamato mia madre per dirle che stavo bene.
As soon as I arrived at the station, I called my mother to tell her I was fine.
Key point: The conjunction che is a chameleon — it takes the indicativo after verbs expressing certainty (so che viene), but the congiuntivo after verbs expressing doubt, desire, or emotion (spero che venga). The mood depends on the main verb, not on che itself.
Subordinating Conjunctions — Congiuntivo
THE KEY SECTION: these conjunctions always trigger the congiuntivo (subjunctive) in the dependent clause. Mastering this list is essential.
Why Do These Trigger the Congiuntivo?
The congiuntivo expresses uncertainty, purpose, concession, or unreality. These conjunctions introduce clauses where the action is not presented as a straight fact — it may be a goal (purpose), something contrary to expectation (concession), a hypothetical scenario (condition), or an event that has not yet happened (anteriority). Whenever the conjunction frames the clause as non-factual, the congiuntivo is required.
Conjunctions That Require Congiuntivo
Conjunction Meaning Category Example with Congiuntivo
affinché / perché so that, in order that purpose Parlo piano affinché tutti capiscano
benché although, even though concessive Esce benché piova a dirotto
sebbene although, even though concessive Sebbene sia stanco, continua a lavorare
nonostante (che) despite, in spite of concessive Nonostante faccia freddo, va al parco
a meno che (non) unless conditional-exclusive Vengo a meno che non piova
prima che before temporal Partiamo prima che arrivi la pioggia
senza che without modal-exclusive È uscito senza che nessuno lo vedesse
purché / a patto che / a condizione che provided that, on condition that conditional Ti aiuto purché tu mi dica la verità
nel caso in cui in case conditional Porta l'ombrello nel caso in cui piova
come se as if comparative-hypothetical Parla come se sapesse tutto (always imperfetto or trapassato congiuntivo)
Examples with Congiuntivo Highlighted
affinché Il professore ripete la spiegazione affinché tutti gli studenti capiscano bene la lezione.
The professor repeats the explanation so that all the students understand the lesson well. (congiuntivo presente)
benché Benché fosse molto stanco dopo il viaggio, ha deciso di uscire a cena con gli amici.
Although he was very tired after the trip, he decided to go out to dinner with friends. (congiuntivo imperfetto)
sebbene Sebbene il tempo non sia dei migliori, la gita al lago resta confermata per sabato.
Although the weather is not the best, the lake trip remains confirmed for Saturday. (congiuntivo presente)
a meno che Andremo al concerto a meno che non venga cancellato a causa della pioggia forte.
We will go to the concert unless it gets cancelled because of the heavy rain. (congiuntivo presente)
prima che Devo finire tutto il lavoro prima che il mio capo arrivi in ufficio domani mattina.
I must finish all the work before my boss arrives at the office tomorrow morning. (congiuntivo presente)
senza che È riuscita a uscire di casa senza che i suoi genitori la sentissero scendere le scale.
She managed to leave the house without her parents hearing her go down the stairs. (congiuntivo imperfetto)
purché Puoi usare la mia macchina purché tu la riporti con il serbatoio pieno entro sera.
You can use my car provided that you bring it back with a full tank by evening. (congiuntivo presente)
come se Gianni parla dell'argomento come se fosse un esperto, ma in realtà non sa quasi nulla.
Gianni talks about the topic as if he were an expert, but in reality he knows almost nothing. (congiuntivo imperfetto)
perché — dual personality: When perché means "because" (explaining a reason), it takes the indicativo: Studio perché voglio imparare. When it means "so that" (expressing purpose), it takes the congiuntivo: Studio perché i miei genitori siano fieri di me. Ask yourself: reason (indicativo) or goal (congiuntivo)?
come se — special rule: This conjunction always requires the congiuntivo imperfetto or trapassato, even when the main verb is in the present tense. You will never see come se + congiuntivo presente. Compare: Parla come se sapesse tutto (He speaks as if he knew everything).
Correlative Conjunctions (Congiunzioni Correlative)
Paired conjunctions that work together to link parallel elements in a sentence. They add balance and emphasis.
Common Pairs
Italian Pair English Type Example
sia...sia / sia...che both...and copulative Parlo sia l'italiano sia il francese
o...o either...or disjunctive O vieni con noi o resti a casa
né...né neither...nor negative-copulative Non bevo né caffè né tè
non solo...ma anche not only...but also copulative-emphatic Non solo cucina, ma prepara anche il dolce
tanto...quanto as much...as comparative Legge tanto quanto scrive ogni giorno
Examples
sia...sia Il nuovo progetto riguarda sia la ricerca scientifica sia lo sviluppo tecnologico dell'azienda.
The new project concerns both the scientific research and the technological development of the company.
o...o Questo fine settimana o andiamo in montagna a fare escursioni o restiamo in città a visitare i musei.
This weekend either we go to the mountains hiking or we stay in the city to visit the museums.
né...né Mia nonna non usa né il computer né il telefono cellulare per comunicare con la famiglia.
My grandmother uses neither the computer nor the mobile phone to communicate with the family.
non solo...ma anche Questo ristorante non solo serve piatti eccellenti, ma offre anche una vista panoramica sulla città.
This restaurant not only serves excellent dishes, but also offers a panoramic view of the city.
tanto...quanto Lucia è tanto brava in matematica quanto suo fratello maggiore che studia ingegneria al politecnico.
Lucia is as good at mathematics as her older brother who studies engineering at the polytechnic.
sia...sia vs sia...che: Both forms are correct. sia...sia is considered more elegant and is preferred in formal writing. sia...che is more common in everyday speech. Avoid mixing them: do not write sia...e.
Common Confusion Points
Several conjunctions change meaning or require a different mood depending on context. These are the pairs that trip up learners most often.
Contrasting Pairs
Conjunction Meaning A (+ Mood) Meaning B (+ Mood) How to Tell
perché because — indicativo so that — congiuntivo Reason = indicativo; Purpose/goal = congiuntivo
se if (real) — indicativo if (unreal) — congiuntivo Possible condition = indicativo; Contrary-to-fact = congiuntivo imperfetto
anche se even if/though (real) — indicativo — Always indicativo; never triggers congiuntivo
sebbene / benché — although — congiuntivo Always congiuntivo; same meaning as "anche se" but different mood
mentre while (temporal) — indicativo — Temporal (simultaneous actions); not used for contrast in formal Italian
invece — instead, on the other hand — indicativo Pure contrast between two facts; never temporal
Contrastive Examples
perché + ind. Studio l'italiano perché voglio trasferirmi a Roma l'anno prossimo per lavoro.
I study Italian because I want to move to Rome next year for work. (reason = indicativo)
perché + cong. Studio l'italiano perché i miei colleghi mi capiscano quando parlo durante le riunioni.
I study Italian so that my colleagues understand me when I speak during meetings. (purpose = congiuntivo)
se + ind. Se domani fa bel tempo, andiamo tutti insieme a fare una passeggiata al lago.
If the weather is nice tomorrow, we all go together for a walk by the lake. (real possibility = indicativo)
se + cong. Se io fossi ricco, comprerei una bella casa con il giardino in campagna toscana.
If I were rich, I would buy a beautiful house with a garden in the Tuscan countryside. (unreal = congiuntivo)
anche se + ind. Anche se è molto tardi e sono stanchissimo, devo finire di scrivere questa relazione importante.
Even though it is very late and I am exhausted, I must finish writing this important report. (real = indicativo)
sebbene + cong. Sebbene sia molto tardi e io sia stanchissimo, devo finire di scrivere questa relazione.
Although it is very late and I am exhausted, I must finish writing this report. (same meaning, congiuntivo required)
mentre Mentre lui guardava la televisione in salotto, lei preparava la cena in cucina.
While he was watching television in the living room, she was preparing dinner in the kitchen. (temporal)
invece Marco preferisce il mare; sua sorella, invece, preferisce la montagna per le vacanze estive.
Marco prefers the sea; his sister, on the other hand, prefers the mountains for summer holidays. (contrast)
anche se vs sebbene/benché: They translate almost identically into English ("although / even though"), but they require different moods. Anche se always takes the indicativo. Sebbene and benché always take the congiuntivo. If in doubt, anche se + indicativo is the safer spoken choice — native speakers use it far more often.
Conjunctions in Everyday Speech
Spoken Italian relies on a set of informal connectors that rarely appear in grammar books but are essential for sounding natural.
Common Spoken Connectors
Connector Meaning / Function Register Usage Note
allora so, well, then informal / neutral Conversation filler and topic launcher: Allora, cosa facciamo stasera?
comunque anyway, in any case informal / neutral Resumes a topic or dismisses the previous point: Comunque, ti volevo dire una cosa
insomma in short, basically, well informal Summarises or expresses mild frustration: Insomma, non lo so!
praticamente basically, practically very informal Overused filler in spoken Italian; avoid in writing: Praticamente è successo che...
tipo like, sort of very informal / youth Similar to English "like" as a filler; avoid in formal contexts
magari maybe, perhaps, if only informal / neutral Expresses wish or possibility: Magari potessimo restare più a lungo!
Register Differences
Formal writing: Uses tuttavia, sebbene, benché, affinché, dunque, pertanto — conjunctions that signal educated prose.
Everyday speech: Prefers però, anche se, allora, comunque, insomma, magari — shorter, punchier connectors.
Rule of thumb: If you see accented endings (-ché) or Latin-derived forms, it is formal. If it sounds like something a friend would say at a bar, it is informal.
Examples
allora Allora, ieri sera sono andato al cinema con Giulia e abbiamo visto quel film di cui ti parlavo.
So, last night I went to the cinema with Giulia and we saw that movie I was telling you about.
comunque Non mi è piaciuto molto il primo piatto; comunque, il dolce alla fine era veramente squisito.
I did not like the first course much; anyway, the dessert at the end was truly exquisite.
insomma Ha parlato per mezz'ora senza arrivare al punto; insomma, non ho capito cosa volesse dire.
He talked for half an hour without getting to the point; in short, I did not understand what he meant.
praticamente Praticamente abbiamo camminato per tre ore sotto la pioggia senza trovare il ristorante giusto.
Basically we walked for three hours in the rain without finding the right restaurant.
magari Magari potessimo tornare in vacanza in Sardegna quest'estate, ma non abbiamo abbastanza soldi.
If only we could go back on holiday to Sardinia this summer, but we do not have enough money.
Tip for learners: Do not avoid informal connectors — they are what make your Italian sound alive. But be aware of context: using tipo and praticamente in an essay or formal email will sound out of place, just as affinché would sound stiff at a dinner table.
Remember: The conjunction itself often determines the mood of the verb in the subordinate clause. When in doubt, check whether the conjunction expresses a fact (indicativo) or a purpose, doubt, concession, or hypothesis (congiuntivo).
